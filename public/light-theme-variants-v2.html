Light Theme Variants v2 — Based on Variant E (Dual-Accent)

Baseline: Variant E's dual-accent (purple + Aerchain orange), gradient canvas, left-border KPI accents. These 5 explore solid color canvases, different glass effects, and deeper/more vibrant backgrounds.

E1 — Soft Lilac Canvas

Solid lilac canvas (hsl 262, 30%, 92%) replacing the gradient. Cards use frosted white glass at 90% opacity with a purple-tinted inset shadow. The purple canvas gives the entire app a cohesive brand feel while white glass cards pop clearly. Sidebar has a stronger frosted effect.

E2 — Deep Navy Canvas

Solid dark navy (hsl 225, 35%, 22%) canvas — almost like a "dark-but-blue" mode. White text, high contrast. Cards use translucent white glass at 8% opacity. Both accents glow against the dark blue. A professional, corporate-premium aesthetic that's neither pure dark nor pure light.

E3 — Lavender Mist + Heavy Glass

Soft lavender canvas (hsl 265, 40%, 90%) with thick frosted glass cards at 70% white opacity and strong blur(30px). Cards have elevated shadows that make them float. The glass effect is the star here — surfaces feel like frosted crystal panes over a tinted backdrop. Purple-dominant with orange as the spark color.

E4 — Midnight Indigo

Deep indigo-purple solid canvas (hsl 255, 30%, 16%). This is a "colored dark mode" — dark but distinctly purple, not the neutral black of the standard dark theme. Orange and purple both pop vibrantly. Cards use 5% white glass. Sidebar active state glows. All accent colors are boosted for maximum vibrancy.

E5 — Warm Violet Gradient + Solid White Cards

A warm-toned violet gradient canvas (lavender → blush) with fully opaque white cards for maximum contrast. No transparency on cards — just clean white with colored left-border accents and crisp shadows. The warmth comes from the canvas and orange accent, while cards feel "on top" and distinct. Best readability of all variants.
